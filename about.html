Artist Statement 2021

I believe there are no fake memories, since the nature of memory is something that, though derived from realism, grows each day further beyond realism. That matches my own interpretation of photography too. My camera is my telescope and weapon, through which I see and shoot from both the present and the past. Being caught in between the times, photography proves fake memories of mine exist.

I grew up in the 90s, and I have always had secret, golden, and vague memories related to that era’s aesthetics stored in some corner of my mind. Most of them are about the places I had never been to when I was a kid. Yet by getting to them through TV and magazines, I established my own escape-land in my childhood bedroom. Such is my personal feeling towards Hong Kong, and many other places too.

I am as much alienated from those places as I am familiar with them. I walk under the morning and afternoon sun across different districts, through different buildings, in different streets until I finally reunite with the moment, when the angle of beams of sunlight through the window, or the combination of different shades of blue of the buildings, or a still point in the downtown hustling, is exactly like how it has been in my memories.

I call it nostalgia for the places I didn’t grow up in, but grew up with. The still moments captured by rolls and rolls of film offer both verification and falsification, a space for meditation and a land for treasure hunt. My works interpret my feelings in light and color, and above all, it serves as the path, which leads my ultimate escape to the eternal rose garden.
